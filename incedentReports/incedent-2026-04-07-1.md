Incident: 2026-04-07 18-33-38 UTC

## Summary

From 18:33 UTC to 18:40 UTC on 2026-04-07, pizza orders were failing with HTTP 500 errors. This was caused by the assigned chaos event in the Pizza Factory dependency. Grafana OnCall caught it, and I treated it as a moderate incident since ordering was down until I fixed it.

The whole thing lasted about 6 minutes. After the alert came in, I acknowledged it, reproduced the failure in the site debugger, checked the failed order response, and used the recovery link in that response to get ordering working again.

## Detection

I found this because Grafana OnCall fired and sent me both a push notification and an email.

Detection was pretty quick. One thing that would help next time is having a dashboard panel and alert that point more directly to order failures and Pizza Factory failures so the cause is obvious right away.

## Impact

For about 6 minutes, from 18:33 UTC to 18:40 UTC on 2026-04-07, anyone trying to place a pizza order could get an HTTP 500 error.

This affected the pizza ordering flow. I do not have an exact number of users impacted, and I did not see any support tickets or other outside reports about it.

## Response

I started responding as soon as the OnCall alert came in at 18:33 UTC and acknowledged it at 18:34 UTC.

I investigated by reproducing the failure in the site debugger, looking at the response body from the failed order request, and following the recovery link that was included there. Once I looked at the response body, the fix was pretty straightforward.

## Root cause

The root cause was the injected chaos event in the Pizza Factory dependency. That caused order requests to fail until the recovery link was used.

## Resolution

I restored service by opening the recovery link in the failed order response body. After that, the order failures stopped and the alert cleared.

I could probably cut the mitigation time down a little more in the future by checking the response body right away any time order requests start returning 500 errors from the factory path.

## Prevention

This was an expected chaos-test failure, not some unexpected bug. The main thing to do going forward is make sure the alerting, dashboard, and debugging workflow stay easy to use so this kind of issue can be recognized and fixed quickly next time.

## Action items

1. Keep the order-failure and factory-failure Grafana alerts enabled for future incidents.
2. Keep the dashboard panels for order failures and related logs easy to access during on-call response.
3. Document that the failed order response body may contain the recovery link needed to resolve this chaos event.
